Brick Work

Work completed during the final year of my degree at Manchester Art School (UK), 2012-2013.

Exhibited at Oddest Bar (Manchester), 2013

Red brickwork is a building method and material evocative of Manchester's productive industrial age, and now a more traditional method of construction compared to that found in inner city design. This work juxtaposes two historic techniques, the brick becoming a rough textural surface that contrasts the clarity of white stitched pattern and laser cut acrylic. The clear Perspex is a pre-cut frame work, offering decorative support for the stitched pattern and subverting hand stitch's typical textile associations. The mixture of materials and folk aesthetic created with hand stitch, reinforce the possibility for embroidery skills to be practiced and installed in modern urban interior and exterior environments.
